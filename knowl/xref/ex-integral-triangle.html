Example 4.3.7.

Evaluate \(\displaystyle\int_1^5 (2-x)\,dx\)
🔗
Solution.
Let’s quickly sketch the graph of \(f(x)=2-x\) to understand the region described by the integral.
🔗
Notice that the graph forms a triangle above the \(x\)-axis on the interval \([1,2]\) and a triangle below the \(x\)-axis on the interval \([2,5]\text{.}\) Therefore:
🔗
The positive part represents accumulation in one direction, while the negative part represents accumulation in the opposite direction. Since the negative portion is larger in magnitude, the overall accumulation on \([1,5]\) will be negative.
🔗
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_1^5 (2-x)\,dx \amp = \text{(area of triangle above)} - \text{(area of triangle below)}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}(1)(1) - \frac{1}{2}(3)(3) \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{9}{2} = -4. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
