Remark 4.2.3. Approximations and Accuracy.

In general the more pieces we break the interval into (the more information we have), the better our estimate will be.
🔗
One could imagine that if we had infinitely velocity readings on microscopic intervals, our approximation could become so accurate that it would be indistinguishable from the exact value. This is a nice idea, but it would be extremely difficult to calculate by hand, so for this section we will stick with approximations and in the next section we will tackle how to calculate exact values.
